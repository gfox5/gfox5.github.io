Butterflies

All information and images on this page were sourced from the Butterfly Wikipedia page.

Butterflies are winged insects from the lepidopteran superfamily Papilionoidea, characterized by large, often brightly coloured wings that often fold together when at rest, and a conspicuous, fluttering flight.

Biology

  • Complete Metamorphosis: Butterflies undergo complete metamorphosis, which involves four distinct stages: egg, larva (caterpillar), pupa (chrysalis), and adult butterfly.

  • Anatomy: Butterflies have a three-part body: head, thorax, and abdomen. They possess a pair of antennae for sensing, compound eyes for vision, and six legs and four wings attached to the thorax. The wings are covered in tiny scales that create their vibrant colors and patterns.

  • Ecology: Butterflies play an important role as pollinators, helping plants reproduce by transferring pollen between flowers. They are also a food source for predators like birds, lizards, and snakes. Butterflies utilize various defense mechanisms, including camouflage, warning coloration, and mimicking the appearance of other objects or animals to deter predators.

  • Lifespan: The average lifespan of an adult butterfly is relatively short, often a few weeks, though some species, like Monarchs, can live for several months, especially during migration.
